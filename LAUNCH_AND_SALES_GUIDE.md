### 2.1 Rules to follow
- Read r/sportsbook rules **before** posting. Typical: no direct link to paid products in post body; value-first; no “buy my thing” as the main point.
- **Safe approach:** Post value (method + screenshot), link in comments only when someone asks or in a single follow-up comment.

### 2.2 Post template (value-first, no link in title/body)

**Title — A/B test (use A first; use B for Week 2 or second post):**
- **Version A (method-focused):** `How I calculate EV and unit size for NBA player props (simple method)`
- **Version B (outcome-focused):** `NBA props: the one sheet I use to decide bet vs pass and how much`

**Body:**
```
I’ve been tracking NBA player props and got tired of doing EV in my head or in a messy spreadsheet. Here’s the method I use now — maybe it helps someone.

**What I do:**
1. Get the line (e.g. 24.5 points, -110).
2. Decide my “fair” probability (e.g. I think he goes over 58% of the time).
3. Convert that to EV. If EV &gt; 0, I bet; if not, I pass.
4. Size the bet by a fixed % of bankroll (or a simple unit scale) so I’m not overbetting.

I put this into one Google Sheet so I’m not recalculating every time: I paste the line, enter my edge, and it tells me EV, recommended units, and bet/pass.
```
