## Page 5 — Edge % Explained (Your Method)

**What “Your Edge %” means**  
- The **Implied Prob** in the sheet is the book’s implied probability from the odds (e.g. +110 → ~47.6%).  
- **Your Edge %** is how much *more* likely you think the outcome is than the book. So if the book implies 47.6% and you think the true probability is 52.6%, your edge is 5 percentage points → enter **5** in **Your Edge %**.

**How to get your edge**  
- **Model:** build a simple model (e.g. last 10 games, matchup, pace) and compare your fair probability to the book.  
- **Closing line value (CLV):** if you bet before the line moves and the close is sharper, CLV can approximate edge over the open.  
- **Consensus + adjustment:** use consensus lines and adjust for injury/rest/pace; the gap between your number and the book is your edge.  
- **Conservative rule:** if you’re unsure, use a smaller edge (e.g. 2–3%). The sheet will show “PASS” or “1u” more often and protect your bankroll.

**Why manual input**  
- You stay in control of your edge. The sheet doesn’t pull lines or “tell” you your edge; it only calculates EV and units from *your* inputs. That keeps the process honest and flexible.

## Page 6 — Kelly Sizing Rules ($600 → 1–2% Risk Max)

**How the sheet sizes bets**  
- The **Kelly Units** column uses a Kelly-style fraction: EV% / (decimal odds - 1), capped at 5% of bankroll so you never go full Kelly.  
- **Recommendation** simplifies to:  
  - **EV &gt; 3%** → “2u BET”  
  - **EV 1–3%** → “1u BET”  
  - **EV &lt; 1%** → “PASS”

**$600 bankroll example**  
- Treat 1u = 1% of bankroll ($6), 2u = 2% ($12).  
- **Max risk per bet:** 2% ($12).  
- **Rough daily cap:** 5–8 plays × 1–2u = 5–16u per day; at 1% that’s $30–96. Stay on the lower end until you’re confident in your edge.

**Rules of thumb**  
- Never bet more than 2u on a single prop from this sheet.  
- If you have a bigger edge than the sheet assumes, you can round up (e.g. 1.5u → 2u) but don’t exceed 2u per play.  
- If EV% is negative or “PASS,” skip the bet.

## Page 8 — Scaling: Multi-Leg + Arb Opportunities

**Single props first**  
- Get comfortable with the EV Calculator and your edge % for single props before adding parlays or arbitrage.

**Multi-leg (parlays)**  
- The sheet is built for single props. For parlays, the math is different (multiply probabilities, different EV). Use the sheet to find +EV legs, then use a parlay calculator elsewhere to combine them and size the parlay small (e.g. 0.5u) until you’re confident.

**Arbitrage**  
- If two books have different lines (e.g. over 4.5 at +110 and under 4.5 at +105 on another book), you can lock in profit. The sheet can show EV per side; arb is when both sides are +EV in their own market. Track each leg in the sheet and size the total stake so you’re equal-profit on both outcomes.

## Page 9 — FAQ

**Why manual input?**  
So you own your edge. The sheet doesn’t scrape lines or guess your edge; you decide. That keeps the process clear and adaptable to any book or line source.

**Where do I get “edge” from?**  
From your own model, CLV, or adjusted consensus. Start conservative (2–3% edge) and increase only when you have evidence.

**Can I use this for other sports?**  
The math is the same (American odds → implied prob, EV, Kelly). Use it for NFL, MLB, etc.; only the stat names and line types change.

**Why 2u max?**  
To limit variance and avoid overbetting when edge is misestimated. You can change the thresholds in the sheet if you use different unit rules.

**What if my book has different odds?**  
Always enter the odds you actually get. The sheet uses whatever you put in.
