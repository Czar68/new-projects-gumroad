*   **Primary Background:** A very dark charcoal gray (#1A1A1A) or a soft, neutral light gray (#F5F5F5). These colors provide maximum contrast and make the 3D model the absolute focal point without visual competition.
*   **Accent Colors:** A single, subtle accent color (like a muted cyan, blue, or orange) used sparingly for UI elements, wireframe overlays, or highlights. This adds a touch of modern tech aesthetic.

### **Composition & Layout**
The image should be a **composite of multiple views and breakdowns in a single, well-organized frame**, similar to a product specification sheet.

**1. Primary Hero Shot (Largest Element):**
*   A dynamic, three-quarter view of the character in a neutral "T-pose" or a very subtle "A-pose."
*   Rendered with studio lighting (3-point lighting) to clearly define anatomy, volume, and silhouette.
*   The character should be centered, with ample negative space around it.

**2. Supporting Visuals (Arranged around the hero shot):**
*   **Turnaround Silhouettes:** A row of 4-6 small, evenly spaced silhouettes of the character from front, side, ╛, and back, presented on a pure white background.
*   **Topology Wireframe Overlay:** A key detail shot (inset) showing a close-up of the face or hand with a clean, quad-dominant wireframe overlaid on the model. This is crucial for technical buyers.
*   **Texture/UV Sheet Display:** A small, neat display of the texture maps (Diffuse/Albedo, Normal, Roughness/Metallic) tiled together to show their organization and resolution.
*   **Posed Example (Optional but valuable):** A smaller render of the character in a simple, appealing pose to demonstrate rigging and deformation quality.

**3. UI & Text Elements (Integrated into the composition):**
*   Clean, sans-serif typography (e.g., Helvetica, Roboto) for labels like "T-POSE," "TOPOLOGY," "TEXTURE SET (4K)," "QUAD-ONLY MESH."
*   Minimal use of thin lines or grids in the background to suggest a digital workspace.

### **What Should Be Shown (Key Selling Points)**

*   **Model Integrity:** Flawless geometry with no visible artifacts. Smooth, deformable surfaces.
*   **Clean Topology:** The wireframe overlay must show edge loops flowing correctly for animation (especially around eyes, mouth, and joints).
*   **Full Body & Proportions:** The character is clearly full-body and anatomically correct (or stylized consistently).
*   **Neutral, Universal Design:** The character should be gender-neutral and stylistically versatile (realistic base or a clean, stylized form) to appeal to the broadest audience.
*   **Technical Readiness:** The inclusion of the UV layout and texture samples proves it's production-ready.
*   **Polygon Count & Stats:** A small, discreet text block stating: e.g., "Polygons: 25,000 tris | Textures: 4K PBR | Rigged: Yes".
